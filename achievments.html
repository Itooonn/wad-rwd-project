Discover Schools: History & Achievements

La Verdad Christian College

Achievements
  • TOP PERFORMING SCHOOL With 10 to 50 Examinees and with 100% passing percentage (SEPTEMBER 2025, Social Worker Licensure Exam)
  • LVCC is the first private school in the Philippines to grant full scholarship programs to deserving students, which includes tuition-free education, no miscellaneous fees, and other support like meals, uniforms, books, etc.
  • A faculty member, Mr. Daniel John Saballa, from the Bachelor of Science in Information Systems (BSIS) and Associate in Computer Technology (ACT) programs, successfully earned a Cisco Certification in Ethical Hacking in July 2025.
History

Established in Apalit in 1998; became a college in 2005.

ACLC College

Achievements
  • In 2025, students from the Tourism Management program passed Front Office Services NC II and Food & Beverage Services NC II assessments.
  • They held celebratory recognition for those students (hospitality sector readiness).
  • The ACLC Apalit listing confirms its location (McArthur Highway, Sulipan, Apalit) and that it offers hospitality / tourism / technical‐vocational credentials.
History

ACLC is part of the ACLC / AMA Education System (private).

DHVSU (Don Honorio Ventura State University) Apalit Campus

Achievements
  • The Okada Academic Building (P50 million donation) was built, 3 floors / 12 classrooms etc. for the Apalit campus.
  • Apalit campus also benefits from the law (RA 12148 / 12147) that converted DHVSU into Pampanga State University, which may bring more resources, recognition etc.
History

Was founded on November 4, 1861, as the Escuela de Artes y Oficios de Bacolor

Our Lady of Fatima University, Pampanga Campus

Achievements
  • In May 2025, two alumni from OLFU Pampanga were topnotchers in the Philippine Nurses Licensure Examination: 6th place (Jemuel Kyle Bejar) with 88.80 %, among others.
  • Also in 2025, OLFU Pampanga’s College of Pharmacy won the People’s Choice Award for Digital Poster (Professional Category) at the 10th Central Luzon Health Research Forum; and its Medical Laboratory Science team got 2nd Place in undergrad oral research.
  • In IT / Computer Studies, OLFU Pampanga won several awards at the IRCITE 2025 regional IT education conference: Best Research Paper, Champion in Device Configuration Management, and 2nd in Technical Writing.
History

OLFU Pampanga is ISO 9001:2015 certified.

DHVSU Santo Tomas Campus

Achievements
  • Sto. Tomas campus is set to open medical and allied health courses: Nursing (initially), then by 2024/2025 Pharmacy, Medical Technology, and even a Doctor of Medicine program. This is a big expansion.
  • Sto. Tomas Campus has recently signed MOAs (Memoranda of Agreement) on ICT and digitalization with local elementary schools / barangay councils (e.g. the “Beyond the Classroom Walls” program with San Matias Elementary School) to extend ICT training, video editing, productivity tools etc.
History

In 2025, the university was renamed “Pampanga State University” under Republic Acts 12147 & 12148, consolidating DHVSU’s campuses and expanding its charter.

ATEC Technological College

Achievements
  • Participation in community / livelihood training: For example, in 2024, ATEC collaborated with the National Housing Authority (NHA) to train beneficiaries (resettlement/homeowners) in “agricrop production” (vegetable gardening etc.), teaching agriculture skills that help livelihood.
  • The Apalit branch continues to be accredited and offering a variety of short-courses and strands like ICT, ABM, HUMSS, etc., enabling NC II / NC III certifications. (While this is more ongoing than a single event, it is relevant).
History

Founded in 1998. It is non-profit, non-stock, non-denominational. Offers technical/vocational / strands / certificate / short courses. Accredited by DepEd, TESDA, DSWD. It has a branch in Apalit, Pampanga.

University of the Assumption (UA)

Achievements
  • Also the design and deployment of a Barangay Constituents Information System (BCIS) that automates issuance of barangay clearance and documents — used in 21 barangays.
  • UA hosts the AOCPA (Assumption Outstanding Campus Press Awards). In 2025, Gordon College’s “The Forefront” won several awards at the 5th AOCPA held at UA. That shows UA as a hub or venue for regional journalism excellence.
History

Founded on January 12, 1963 as Assumption Junior College, by Bishop Emilio Cinense and Msgr. Pedro Puno. Initial programs included Bachelor of Arts, Bachelor of Science in Commerce, BS Education, BS Elementary Education.
